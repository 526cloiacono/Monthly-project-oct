MLB Career

Boston Red Sox: 1915–19

Playing for the Red Sox, Ruth soon became the best left-handed pitcher in baseball. Between 1915 and 1919 he won 87 games and won three World Series games. During a streak of scoreless World Series innings, he also set a record by pitching 292/3 consecutive shutout innings.

New York Yankees: 1920–34

As a full-time outfielder with the Yankees, Ruth quickly established his claim on being the greatest hitter to have ever played the game. in his first season with the Yankees in 1920, he shattered his own single-season record by hitting 54 home runs, 25 more than he had hit in 1919.

Boston Braves: 1935

After 1932 Ruth’s playing skills rapidly diminished. His offensive numbers dropped sharply. He wanted to manage the Yankees, but Ruppert, the team’s owner, is reported to have said that Ruth could not control his own behavior, let alone that of the other players, and so refused to offer him the post.
